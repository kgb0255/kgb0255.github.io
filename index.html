I'm a Physics Ph.D. candidate at the University of California, Santa Barbara, advised by Prof. Tejaswi Venumadhav. Prior to UCSB, I obtained my Bachelor's degrees in Physics and Astrophysics from UC Berkeley.

My work focuses on the nonlinear tidal response of neutron stars during binary inspiral. When two neutron stars orbit each other, tidal forces distort each star and excite internal oscillations. During the late stages of inspiral, these effects become nonlinear, and I calculate that nonlinearity from first principles by extending perturbation theory to higher order.

Outside of work, I enjoy playing the piano, training in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, and traveling to new countries!
